# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students:

## Do's ✅

1. Keep your profiles professional — Use your real name and a decent profile photo on platforms like LinkedIn. Employers and internship coordinators do look at these.

2. Think before you post — Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with your professor or a future employer seeing what you are about to post.

3. Use strong and unique passwords — Enable two factor authentication on all your social media accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

4. Give credit when sharing others' work — If you share someone else's photo, article or idea, always tag or mention the original creator.

5. Keep personal information private — Review your privacy settings regularly and limit who can see your personal details like phone number, location and address.

6. Report harmful or suspicious content — If you come across cyberbullying, fake accounts or scam links, use the platform's report feature.


## Don'ts ❌

1. Don't share your location in real time — Posting that you are away from home or sharing your live location publicly can be a safety risk.

2. Don't engage with strangers asking for personal details — Scammers often pose as peers, companies or recruiters to extract sensitive information.

3. Don't post during exams or college hours — It reflects poorly on your discipline and can be used against you in academic or professional settings.

4. Don't spread unverified news or rumours — Always check the source before sharing any news, especially during sensitive events.

5. Don't use social media to vent about college, professors or classmates — What feels like a private rant can spread quickly and damage your reputation permanently

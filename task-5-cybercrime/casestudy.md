# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## The Incident:
In November 2024, a 67-year-old woman named Bharti Bai Agarwal from Hyderabad received a phone call from fraudsters who falsely claimed her Aadhaar-linked phone number was involved in a money laundering case. The callers identified themselves as CBI officers and manipulated her into connecting via Skype, where they held her and her daughters under "digital house arrest" for 17 days. During this period the family was threatened and psychologically pressured into transferring a total of ₹5.5 crores through UPI and digital payment channels. The Telangana Cyber Security Bureau registered an FIR and launched an investigation into the case.

## How It Happened (Step by Step):
1. The fraudsters called the victim claiming her Aadhaar number was linked to illegal activities.

2. They impersonated CBI officers to create fear and urgency.

3. They moved the conversation to Skype to maintain control and monitor the family.
4. Using threats of arrest they kept the family isolated and under psychological pressure for 17 days.
5. They gradually convinced the family to transfer money via UPI to "clear their name".
6. By the time the family realized it was a scam, the money was gone.

## Who Is Usually Targeted:
Elderly individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems, people who panic easily under threats of legal action, and anyone unaware that no legitimate government agency conducts arrests or investigations over phone or video calls.

## Consequences:
The family lost ₹5.5 crores. Beyond the financial loss, they suffered severe psychological trauma from 17 days of intimidation. The case highlighted how fraudsters combine impersonation, fear tactics and UPI payments to execute large scale fraud.
